Digital Images

Here is a collage I created for my assignment part A.

My Digital Collage using GIMP 2.0

Creating this collage was a multi-step process that involved downloading, organizing, and manipulating various images and text elements using GIMP, a powerful open-source image editing software. Initially, I downloaded the zipped file named “Images_Collage_T12024.zip” provided by the course. This file contained all the necessary images for the collage. I extracted the contents to a designated folder on my computer to ensure that all files were easily accessible and organized for the project.

The first step in GIMP was setting up the canvas. I created a new canvas with the specified dimensions of 600 pixels by 400 pixels, ensuring it matched the required size for the finished collage. This precise dimension was critical as it was part of the assignment's evaluation criteria. With the canvas ready, I moved on to importing the main background image. I selected the mountain image from the extracted files and imported it into GIMP. I resized and positioned the image to cover the entire canvas, setting a visually appealing and appropriate backdrop for the collage.

Next, I added the image of a person in a yoga posture. This image was placed on the left side of the canvas. To blend this image seamlessly with the background, I applied a feather effect. This effect softened the edges of the yoga posture image, creating a smooth transition between the foreground and the background. The use of the feather effect was crucial to avoid harsh edges and to achieve a professional look that closely matched the sample collage provided in the assignment guidelines.

The addition of my student ID number was the next significant step. Using the Text Tool in GIMP, I typed “MIT231423” and positioned it at the bottom center of the collage. To adhere to the assignment's specifications, I set the font-family to Goudy Stout, the font size to 42 points, and the font color to white. This specific font choice and size were essential to replicate the appearance of the sample collage accurately. Additionally, I needed to add a white background behind the text to make it stand out clearly against the colorful and detailed background image.

To achieve the white background for the text, I created a new layer beneath the text layer in the Layers dialog. I then selected the text area by right-clicking on the text layer and choosing the "Alpha to Selection" option. This created a selection around the text. To ensure the background extended beyond the text, I used the "Grow" selection tool, expanding the selection by 10 pixels. With the selection area properly expanded, I chose the Bucket Fill Tool and filled the selection with white on the new layer. This step effectively added a white rectangular background behind the text, making it more legible and visually consistent with the sample provided.

Once all the elements were correctly positioned and styled, I saved the project as a .xcf file. This format preserved all the layers and edits, allowing for future modifications if needed. I then exported the finished collage as a .jpg file, ensuring it maintained the required dimensions of 600 pixels by 400 pixels.

To document the process, I opened Microsoft Word and detailed each step involved in creating the collage. This documentation included downloading and extracting images, setting up the canvas, adding and adjusting images, applying effects, and adding and formatting text. I also included screenshots at each major step to provide a visual guide, making the process clear and replicable.

In conclusion, the creation of this collage involved meticulous attention to detail and adherence to the assignment guidelines. The steps were systematically executed to ensure the final product closely matched the provided sample, demonstrating proficiency in using GIMP and following precise instructions.
